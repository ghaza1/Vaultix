Getting Started

To create a new account in Vaultix, follow these steps:

  1. Click on the "Sign Up" button in the top right corner of the homepage.
  2. Fill out the registration form with your name, email address, and password.
  3. Accept the terms of service and privacy policy.
  4. Click "Create Account" to submit your registration.
  5. Wait for administrator approval (if enabled in your organization).
  6. Once approved, you'll receive an email confirmation with a link to activate your account.

After completing these steps, you can log in and start using Vaultix.

To upload a document to Vaultix:

  1. Navigate to your dashboard after logging in.
  2. Click on the "Upload New Document" button.
  3. Select the file you want to upload from your computer.
  4. Add any necessary metadata or tags to help organize your document.
  5. Choose encryption options if needed.
  6. Click "Upload" to submit the document.
  7. Wait for administrator approval (if required by your organization).

Once uploaded and approved, your document will be securely stored and accessible from your documents list.

To digitally sign a document in Vaultix:

  1. Navigate to your documents list.
  2. Find the document you want to sign and click on it to view details.
  3. Click the "Sign Document" button.
  4. Review the document contents to ensure it's the correct version.
  5. Confirm your identity through the authentication process.
  6. Click "Apply Signature" to digitally sign the document.

After signing, the document will display a "Digitally Signed" badge, and a record of your signature will be stored in the system.

To enhance your account security with two-factor authentication:

  1. Go to your profile settings by clicking on your name in the top right corner.
  2. Select "Security" from the menu.
  3. Click "Enable Two-Factor Authentication."
  4. Scan the displayed QR code with your authenticator app (like Google Authenticator or Authy).
  5. Enter the verification code from your authenticator app to confirm setup.
  6. Save your backup codes in a secure location for emergency access.

Once enabled, you'll need to enter a verification code from your authenticator app each time you log in, providing an extra layer of security for your account.
